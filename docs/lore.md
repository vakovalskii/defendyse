# Lore -- Defendyse

## Setting

Deep space. A lone planetary core -- the last energy source of a dying civilization -- drifts in the void. An endless swarm of organic creatures approaches from the dark: mindless, relentless, hungry for energy. The contrast is stark: cold, precise techno machines defend against writhing biological horrors.

## The Core

The planetary core pulses with ancient energy, its surface crackling with defensive lightning. When enemies draw too close, arcs of electricity leap outward, striking every intruder within reach simultaneously. The core is no passive target -- it fights back, searing all organic matter that breaches its perimeter. But the core's energy is finite, and each impact drains its reserves.

## The Swarm

The organic invaders emerge from the void's edge in three castes:

### Scarab Drones
Small insectoid creatures, their carapaces glinting with bioluminescence. They swarm in vast numbers, each one individually weak but collectively overwhelming. Their bodies detonate on contact with the core, sacrificing themselves to drain its energy. Visually, they resemble armored beetles with trailing antennae, their movement erratic but purposeful.

### Squid Fighters
Larger predators with elongated, tentacled forms. Unlike the mindless drones, fighters possess rudimentary intelligence -- they slow their approach to hurl venomous projectiles at the core from range before closing in for the kill. Their bioluminescent patterns pulse in aggressive reds as they fire, their shots arcing through space like toxic spores.

### Crab Tanks
Massive crustacean behemoths, their shells layered thick with chitinous armor. They advance slowly but inevitably, absorbing enormous punishment before they fall. When a tank reaches the core, the impact is catastrophic -- a single breach can drain more energy than a dozen drones. Their silhouettes loom like armored fortresses, legs churning methodically through the void.

## The Defense Grid

Against the organic tide stands a network of automated defense stations -- cold geometry and precise engineering.

### Gatling Turret
A compact platform with rotating barrels that spew streams of kinetic rounds. The barrels spin visibly as they fire, spent rounds tracing bright lines through the dark. Cheap to deploy, these turrets form the backbone of any defensive line with their relentless rate of fire.

### Cannon Emplacement
A heavy weapons platform encased in a diamond-shaped frame. Its plasma rounds are massive, glowing orbs that punch through multiple targets without stopping -- the ionized matter simply passes through flesh and chitin, leaving scorched tunnels through anything in its path. Slow to charge but devastating when it fires.

### Laser Array
A crystalline lens assembly that channels focused light into searing beams. The shots are nearly instantaneous -- blink and you miss them. The lens rotates to track targets with mechanical precision, but its effective range is limited by beam coherence.

### Hive Station
A hexagonal structure that manufactures autonomous combat drones. Unlike the other towers, the Hive does not fire directly. Instead, small metallic drones orbit the station, then break formation to engage targets independently. They harry enemies within the station's control radius, returning to orbit when threats are neutralized. As the station is upgraded, its production capacity grows, fielding ever-larger swarms of mechanical hunters.

## The Pilot

A lone cyber fighter patrols the defense perimeter -- the last human element in an otherwise automated war. The ship darts between enemies, its cyan bolts spraying in widening fans as the pilot gains experience. Near the core, the ship's systems regenerate, drawing on the same energy it defends. The pilot grows stronger with each kill, the ship's neural interface learning from combat. When the ship falls, the core's energy reconstitutes it -- but the seconds spent rebuilding are seconds the swarm advances unchecked.

## Spirit Links

The defense stations can be connected by spirit links -- channels of resonant energy that flow between towers. These ethereal connections amplify both the damage output and firing speed of linked stations, the energy pulsing visibly along glowing conduits. When three stations form a complete triangle of links, the enclosed space fills with a disruptive energy field that slows all organic matter passing through, as if the void itself thickens against the swarm.

## Anomalies -- Black Holes

The fabric of space around the defense zone is unstable. Gravitational anomalies spontaneously form along the zone's borders -- miniature black holes that warp the battlefield. They pull everything nearby toward their center: enemies are dragged off course and destroyed if they cross the event horizon, but towers can be displaced or consumed as well. Projectiles curve through warped spacetime. Drones struggle against the pull. The anomalies are impartial -- they are not allies. They are the void asserting itself.

## The Power Field

The core radiates an energy field that decays with distance. Towers positioned near the core draw heavily from this field, their systems operating at peak efficiency. Those deployed at the outer reaches of the defense zone must operate on minimal power, their damage reduced and their mechanisms sluggish. The tradeoff is fundamental: cluster close for power, spread wide for coverage.

## The Void

Beyond the defense zone, the void stretches infinite. Distant planets drift slowly against the background, their surfaces lifeless. Nebulae glow in muted colors, indifferent to the battle. Parallax layers shift as though the viewer drifts through deep space. The void does not care about the core, the swarm, or the machines. It simply is.
